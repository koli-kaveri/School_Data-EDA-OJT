3. EDA SUMMARY
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Univariate:
  • Scores roughly normally distributed, mean ~60%
  • 3,579 Rural schools vs 1,621 Urban schools (~69% rural)
  • Teacher-Student Ratio: right-skewed; some schools
    are very overcrowded

  Bivariate:
  • Urban schools have consistently higher median scores
  • District_A shows slightly better performance overall
  • Parent literacy rate shows a positive trend with scores

  Correlation Highlights:
  • Math ↔ Language ↔ Science: all highly correlated (>0.7)
  • Parent_Literacy_Rate → Math Score: moderate positive
  • %_Marginalized_Students → Math Score: negative
  • Infrastructure Score → Scores: weak positive

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4. KEY INSIGHTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  1. Urban-Rural Gap: Urban schools score ~5–8% higher on
     average. Better infrastructure and teacher quality
     in urban areas likely explain this.

  2. Parent Literacy Matters: Schools in areas with higher
     parent literacy see better student performance,
     suggesting home support is a key factor.

  3. Infrastructure Gaps: Many schools (especially rural)
     lack internet, electricity, or a library — these
     are strongly linked to lower scores.

  4. Correlated Subjects: A school doing well in Math also
     tends to do well in Language and Science, pointing to
     school-level management as the key driver.

  5. Marginalized Students Disadvantaged: Higher % of
     marginalized students in a school = lower overall
     scores, highlighting a critical equity issue.

  6. Teacher Experience Has Weak Effect: Experience alone
     doesn't strongly predict scores; training and
     infrastructure seem more impactful.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5. MODEL RESULTS (Linear Regression)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Features Used:
  • Avg_Teacher_Experience_Years
  • Teacher_Trainings_Attended
  • Parent_Literacy_Rate
  • Infra_Score (sum of 4 binary features)
  • Is_Urban (1 = Urban)
  • %_Marginalized_Students
  • %_Students_With_Home_Internet

  Results:
  • R² Score  : ~0.20–0.30 (model explains ~20–30% variance)
  • MAE       : ~10–12 points

  Note: Low R² is expected — school performance depends on
  many complex social/policy factors not captured here.
